Ronaldinho | Net Worth and Income

As of 2023, Ronaldinho’s net worth of $90 million. He has amassed this huge fortune through football contracts, endorsements, and investments.

Transfer And Earning From Contracts

Dinho started his football career in Gremio. Afterward, he moved to PSG with an initial transfer fee of £5.96 million.

He played there for three years and transferred to Barcelona with a transfer fee of $37.7 million.

After moving to Barcelona, his salary rose sharply, and he was earning $10 million a year. Similarly, in 2007 he made $31 million through salary and endorsement deals.

Likewise, he moved to AC Milan in 2008 for the transfer fee of $29.23 million. Ronaldinho signed a three-year contract worth $27 million, which would earn him an average of $9 million per year.

Moreover, his total earnings in 2009, 2010, 2011 were $29.2 million, $25 million, and $24.16 million respectively.

In 2011 Ronaldinho made a move to Brazilian club Flamengo with a transfer fee of $3.5 million, where he earned a monthly salary of $190,000.

Legal Troubles

In July 2019, Ronaldinho’s Spanish and Brazilian passports were confiscated due to unpaid fines and taxes.

Moreover, due to tax issues, his properties were also seized.

The tax was reduced from R$ 8.5 million to R$6.5 million because he built a fishing platform in the Guaiba River.

However, he and his brother failed to pay the fine, and their passports were suspended. In March 2020, he went to Paraguay for a charity event but was held under custody for a fake passport.

Moreover, he was kept under house arrest in the luxury hotel of Asuncion after paying a bail of $800,000 each for himself and his brother.

They were released in August 2020 after paying a fine of $90,000 for himself and $110,000 for his brother.

Ronaldinho | Career

Dinho started his club career with Gremio in 1998 and became one of the most notable football prospects after he scored 22 goals in 47 matches just at the age of eighteen.

Afterward, he played for elite clubs like PSG, Barcelona, AC Milan and finally retired on 16 January 2018.

He has played 699 games at the club level scoring 266 goals winning various titles, including Laliga, Serie A, and Champions League.

Similarly, Ronaldinho started his senior international career in Copa America in 1999 against Latvia. He featured in 154 games for Brazil, scoring 62 goals before retiring in 2013.

Dinho has also won many international trophies like Copa America 1999, FIFA World Cup 2002, and FIFA Confederation Cup 2005.
