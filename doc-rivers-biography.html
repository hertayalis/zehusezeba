Doc Rivers: Professional Life, Career, and Awards

Doc began his basketball career playing as a defense at Marquette University. He represented the United States with the national team in the 1982 FIBA World Championship and won a silver medal.

After that, he played for Los Angeles Clippers for a year and played two years for New York Knicks. He played for San Antonio Spurs from 1994 to 1996 and retired as a professional basketball player in 1996.

After three years of his retirement, Rivers began his coaching career with the Orlando Magic in 1999. He won the Coach of the Year award in 2000 after his first year with the club.

He was fired by the club in 2003. After that, he became a commentator for the NBA on ABC and he took over the Boston Celtics coaching position in 2004.

He was criticized for his coaching style and he was publicly called to be fired in 2006. On June 17, 2008, Rivers won his first NBA Championship as a head coach after defeating the Los Angeles Lakers in six games.

He left the club in 2013 and signed for Los Angeles Clippers that year. He also became the Senior Vice President of Basketball Operations on the team.

The Philadelphia 76ers made the announcement that they had signed Rivers as their head coach on October 3, 2020.
