Anita Manning Biography

Anita Manning born on 1 December 1947, is a Scottish antiques expert and television personality from Glasgow well known for her appearances since 2010 as an expert and presenter on the BBC’s Bargain Hunt, Flog It! and the Antiques Road Trip. She is claimed to be the first female auctioneer in Scotland.

Anita Manning’s father always took her to Sauchiehall Street auctions in Glasgow, and she developed a love for antiques. She first entered her antique business after purchasing a bed from an Irish dealer in the 1970s who offered her a job as a buyer.

Anita Manning studied physical education in Aberdeen and Edinburgh, specializing in dance and teaching before marrying and having children. As a young mother, she acquired her knowledge of antiques by buying furniture at auctions. After visiting Glasgow for the first time as a shopper, she spent about three years buying and selling furniture in England and Ireland, driving trucks, and traveling around the country.

Anita Manning and her daughter Lara have run the Glasgow auction house Great Western Auctions since 1989. Her son lives in Hong Kong, but her mother lived the last 35 years of her life in Australia. She was Scotland’s first female auctioneer, according to the Sunday Post.

In 2016 Anita Manning set a new record for the largest profit on a single item on the Antiques Road Trip. A Buddha statue she had purchased for £50 sold for £3,800, a 7,500% profit. This record was broken in September 2017 when Paul Laidlaw bought a Chambre Automatique De Bertsch sub-miniature camera for £60 and sold it at auction for £20,000.
